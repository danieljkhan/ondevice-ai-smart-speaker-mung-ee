# On-Device AI Smart Speaker — mung-ee (뭉이)

> **"세상에서 가장 안전한, 우리 아이의 첫 번째 AI 친구"**
> *"The safest AI friend — a child's very first."*

**mung-ee** is a fully **offline, on-device AI conversation companion** ("smart
speaker") for children under the age of 10. It runs the entire voice pipeline —
wake, listen, understand, reason, speak, and remember — **locally on a single
NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano Super (8 GB)**, with no dependency on the cloud during a
conversation. The design goal is safety first: a child's data never has to leave
the device to hold a conversation.

> ⚠️ **Status: research / educational proof-of-concept (graduate project).**
> This is **not** a certified or commercial product and **not** a safety-certified
> children's device. It is provided *as is* (Apache-2.0, see `LICENSE`). Do not
> deploy with real children without your own independent safety, privacy, and
> legal review.

---

## Why

Most "AI for kids" products stream a child's voice to the cloud. mung-ee asks a
different question: *can a genuinely helpful AI friend run entirely on a small
edge device, so that conversations stay private by construction?* Every design,
safety, and UX decision is evaluated against two tests:

1. Is this the **safest** option for a child?
2. Does it serve the **"first AI friend"** experience?

## How it works

The runtime is a local voice pipeline, all on-device:

```
 mic ─▶ Silero VAD ─▶ Qwen3-ASR (STT) ─▶ llama.cpp / Gemma 4 (LLM)
                                              │
                          safety filters ◀────┤
                                              ▼
        speaker ◀─ Supertonic TTS 2 ◀─ response ◀─ Conversation-Memory RAG
```

- **VAD** — Silero voice-activity detection.
- **STT** — Qwen3-ASR speech-to-text.
- **LLM** — `llama.cpp` (GGUF), primary **Gemma 4 E4B** with an E2B fallback.
- **TTS** — Supertonic TTS 2 (Korean child-friendly voice).
- **Memory** — on-device conversation-memory RAG (sentence embeddings + FAISS),
  with a strict prompt-token budget.
- **Safety** — content filters and child-safe policies gate every response;
  a Korean-history factual-grounding layer reduces hallucination.

## Hardware

- **Compute:** Jetson Orin Nano Super 8 GB (JetPack 6.2 / CUDA 12.6 / Python 3.10)
- **Audio:** USB sound card (mic + speaker)
- **Display:** 4-inch 720×720 capacitive touch IPS LCD, kiosk renderer with
  animated character expressions

## What is *not* in this repository

By design (licensing / privacy / size), the following are **excluded** and must
be supplied or regenerated by the operator — see **`NOTICE`**:

- Model weights (LLM GGUF, STT/TTS/embedding ONNX)
- Custom synthesized TTS voices
- Fine-tuning / RAG corpora derived from external LLM outputs or academic-only
  datasets (e.g. AI-Hub)
- Any child conversation/audio data (never committed; on-device only)
- Runtime secrets (parent-portal PIN, device env) (never committed)

## Getting started (developers)

This repository is the **source tree** for the device; the models and runtime
data live on the Jetson. To explore or work on the code on a dev machine:

```bash
python -m venv .venv && source .venv/bin/activate
pip install -r requirements-dev.txt
ruff check . && mypy core/ models/ safety/ hardware/ scripts/ parental/
pytest tests/
```

Device setup, deployment, and on-Jetson verification are documented under
`docs/runbooks/` (start with `docs/runbooks/jetson-setup-guide.md` and
`docs/runbooks/jetson-deployment-operations.md`). The architectural rationale for
every major decision lives in `docs/adr/`.

## Privacy & safety posture

- Conversations are processed **locally**; nothing is streamed to the cloud to
  hold a conversation.
- Conversation data is stored **on-device only**, for parental review and quality
  improvement, and is never committed to this repository.
- A parent can retrieve conversation logs via a **Tailscale-only** download
  portal (fails closed off the private tailnet; PIN-protected with PBKDF2).
- Responses pass through content-safety filters before they are ever spoken.

These are engineering measures in a research prototype, **not** a compliance
guarantee. See the disclaimer above and `NOTICE`.
